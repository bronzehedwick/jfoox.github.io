What is e-value?

Posted on October 7, 2015

A standard practice for assessing primary homology of a gene sequence is through local alignment (e.g., NCBI’s BLAST), in which an input sequence (the “query”) is matched against a particular sequence (the “hit”) in a database of sequences. The metric most commonly used to determine the significance of an alignment between a query and its hit is e-value.
